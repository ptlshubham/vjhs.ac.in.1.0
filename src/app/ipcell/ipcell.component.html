Intellectual Property (IP) Cell

The Intellectual Property (IP) Cell of Charotar Education Society is established with the objective of fostering awareness, protection, and responsible management of intellectual assets among faculty, students, project staff, supporting staff, and visitors. Recognizing the significance of intellectual property in driving innovation and societal impact, the IP Cell provides guidance and support for safeguarding novel works through patents, industrial designs, copyrights, and trade/service marks.

The IP Cell serves as a resource center to encourage and facilitate creative activities across academic, consultancy, and research domains. It offers individuals the option to seek IP protection for their innovative work or contribute to the public domain, as per their discretion. The Cell also ensures a transparent framework for the ownership, control, and equitable revenue-sharing of intellectual properties owned by Charotar Education Society.

Objectives of the IP Policy
  • Encourage Innovation & Creativity – Foster a culture of research, innovation, and creative expression across disciplines.
  • IP Protection & Management – Provide guidance on securing patents, industrial designs, copyrights, and trademarks while offering the option to keep novel works in the public domain.
  • Fair Ownership & Revenue Sharing – Define a transparent system for ownership rights, control, and distribution of revenues generated from intellectual property owned by CES.
  • Technology Transfer & Commercialization – Facilitate the utilization of intellectual property for societal benefit through licensing, startups, or industry collaborations.
  • Legal Compliance & Ethical Standards – Ensure adherence to national and international IP laws and ethical research practices.
Applicability & Scope
  • This policy applies to all academic and research institutions under Charotar Education Society.
  • Covers faculty, students, project staff, supporting staff, and visitors involved in research, innovation, and creative activities.
  • Governs all forms of intellectual property including patents, copyrights, industrial designs, and trademarks developed under CES.

The IP Cell of CES will oversee the implementation of this policy, ensuring effective IP management, awareness, and support for innovators. Through this initiative, CES aims to contribute meaningfully to the advancement of knowledge, technology, and societal progress.

Through structured initiatives, the IP Cell aims to empower innovators, drive technological advancements, and enhance the institution’s contribution to knowledge-based growth.

Steps for CES Invention Disclosure Process
  • 1. Idea Collection : The institute gathers innovative ideas and inventions from staff and students
  • 2. Form Submission : The CES Invention Disclosure Form is filled out at the institute level, signed, and verified by the Principal.
  • 3. Committee Presentation : The completed CES Invention Disclosure Form is presented in the monthly meeting of the CES IP Cell Committee for review.
  • 4. Committee Remarks : The CES IP Cell Committee provides written remarks on the submission.
  • 5. Final Approval : The Chairperson of the CES IP Cell Committee grants the final approval.
